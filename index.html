Topology & diode schedule
Variable caps (rotors, complementary swing): C1 node 1↔gnd · C2 node 4↔gnd.
Fixed transfer caps: Ca nodes 1↔2 · Cb nodes 3↔4.
Cpar ties each of nodes 1–4 to gnd.
Diodes (anode→cathode, conducting when Vfrom≥Vto):
  D1: 2→0 · D2: 3→0 · D3: 1→3 · D4: 4→2.
Cross-diodes D3/D4 are the regenerative cross-couple — this is what makes the 4-node device pump without needing parasitic asymmetry, unlike the basic 3-node version.
Engine: 2 phases / cycle (C1-meshed, C2-meshed). Each phase: per-node charges conserved from previous phase; brute-search all 16 diode on/off states; keep those consistent with the bias signs (off-diodes must satisfy Vfrom≤Vto); among consistent states take the one maximising |V₁|+|V₄|. Iterate 120 cycles; z = median asymptotic ratio.

Symmetric 4-node Bennet doubler. Cross-diodes D3 (1→3) and D4 (4→2) provide the regenerative feedback path: when C1 swings to its minimum, |V₁| rises and clamps node 3 through D3, which then delivers charge to C2's swing via Cb. The same loop runs the other way through Ca and D4 on the next half-cycle. The device pumps without parasitic seed (no-swing = z=1 exactly, device = z≈1.20 even with small Cpar), unlike the 3-node basic doubler which needs parasitic symmetry-breaking.
